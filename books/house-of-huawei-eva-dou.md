# House of Huawei

## Book Info

- Publisher metadata checked from Penguin Random House.
- This entry tracks the Portfolio hardcover edition.
- Full subtitle: *The Secret History of China's Most Powerful Company*.
- Summary: Eva Dou tells the story of Huawei's rise from a little-known telecom equipment company in southern China into a global technology power. The book centers on founder Ren Zhengfei, Huawei's corporate culture, its international expansion, the role of Chinese state support, and the political crisis that followed the 2018 detention of Meng Wanzhou.
- Why it matters: The book uses Huawei as a lens for understanding China's rise, the U.S.-China technology conflict, national security concerns around telecom infrastructure, and the way private companies can become entangled with state power and geopolitics.
- Why someone might read it: It is useful for understanding how a single company became central to debates about 5G, surveillance, sanctions, supply chains, industrial policy, and technological sovereignty.
- Note on title: The recommended book appears to be *House of Huawei*, not "House of Highway."
