# The Goldfinch

## Book Info

- Publisher metadata checked from Hachette Book Group.
- This entry tracks the Back Bay Books trade paperback edition.
- Hachette lists the book as literary fiction.
- Summary: The novel follows Theo Decker, who survives a catastrophic museum accident that kills his mother and leaves him emotionally bound to a small Dutch painting, Carel Fabritius's *The Goldfinch*. His attachment to the painting pulls him through grief, foster care, wealth, antiques, secrecy, fraud, friendship, addiction, and the art underworld.
- Why it matters: The book won the 2014 Pulitzer Prize for Fiction. Pulitzer described it as a coming-of-age novel about a grieving boy's entanglement with a famous painting that survived destruction, and recognized its emotional and intellectual force.
- Why someone might read it: It is a large literary novel about grief, beauty, obsession, survival, class, fate, and the way art can become a private anchor during a life that feels unstable. Archita may have recommended it because it is immersive, emotionally intense, and widely discussed as a major contemporary novel.
