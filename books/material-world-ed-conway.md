# Material World

## Book Info

- Publisher metadata checked from Penguin Random House Higher Education.
- This entry tracks the Knopf hardcover edition.
- Full subtitle: *The Six Raw Materials That Shape Modern Civilization*.
- Summary: Ed Conway argues that the modern world is not as weightless or digital as it feels. He follows six basic materials: sand, salt, iron, copper, oil, and lithium, showing how they are extracted, refined, transported, and transformed into the infrastructure of everyday life, from fiber-optic cables and power grids to silicon chips, batteries, buildings, and industrial supply chains.
- Why it matters: The book explains the physical foundations behind technology, economics, climate policy, energy transition, and geopolitics. It makes the hidden material systems behind modern life easier to see, especially the mining, manufacturing, logistics, and industrial dependencies that most people usually ignore.
- Why someone might read it: It is useful for understanding why raw materials still shape power, growth, environmental damage, and strategic competition, even in an era that often talks as if software and information are detached from the physical world.
- Note on title: The recommended book appears to be *Material World*, not "Materials."
